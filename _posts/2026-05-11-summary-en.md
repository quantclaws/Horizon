## [Hardware Attestation Enables Vendor Lock‑in and Monopoly Control](https://grapheneos.social/@GrapheneOS/116550899908879585) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The GrapheneOS post argues that hardware attestation mechanisms are being used to tie devices to approved vendors, creating a pathway for monopoly control and undermining user privacy. This trend threatens open ecosystems by forcing reliance on a few silicon vendors, raises concerns about traceability of attestation data, and conflicts with EU digital sovereignty goals such as the EUDI wallet. The post notes that current attestation lacks zero‑knowledge proofs or blind signatures, so each attestation packet can be linked back to the device, and cites historical opposition to Intel CPU serial numbers and Windows 11 TPM requirements as steps toward vendor lock‑in.

hackernews · ChuckMcM · May 10, 17:54 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48086190)

**Background**: Hardware attestation is a cryptographic process that verifies a device’s hardware and firmware are in a known, trusted state, often using TPMs or similar secure elements. Trusted Computing initiatives have promoted such measures to ensure system integrity, but they can also be used to restrict which software or hardware may run on a device. The EU’s proposed Digital Identity Wallet (EUDI) requires attestation from approved vendors, effectively tying digital identities to specific hardware ecosystems.
