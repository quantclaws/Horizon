## [: Research Finds Mainstream AI Answers Bias Toward Japan and US](https://cybernews.com/ai-news/every-ai-answer-japan/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A study by the University of the Basque Country and Cardiff University evaluated eight mainstream large language models across 24 languages, asking 31,680 cultural questions and found that the models frequently anchor their answers to Japan or the United States, with five models showing a stronger bias toward Japan and two toward the US. The findings reveal a systematic cultural bias introduced during supervised fine‑tuning, which can affect the fairness and reliability of AI systems in multilingual settings and highlights the need for more balanced training data. Bias was traced to the supervised fine‑tuning stage, while the base pretrained models remained relatively balanced; low‑resource languages were more likely to produce answers pointing to the model’s own country.

**Background**: Supervised fine‑tuning (SFT) is the process of further training a pretrained model on a labeled dataset to adapt it to a specific task, a common step in developing large language models. Low‑resource languages are those with limited digital text and linguistic resources, making it harder for models to learn accurate representations. Cultural bias in LLMs refers to systematic tendencies in model outputs that reflect the cultural perspectives overrepresented in their training data.

## [:Chinese grey market sells Claude API at 90% off while harvesting data and swapping models](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/chinese-grey-market-sells-claude-api-access-at-90-percent-off-through-proxy-networks-that-harvest-user-data) ⭐️ 8.0/10

An investigation by Oxford China Policy Lab researcher Zilan Qian reveals that Chinese grey‑market proxy services are reselling access to Anthropic’s Claude models at as little as 10% of the official price, while collecting users’ prompts and outputs and often substituting cheaper or domestic models for the promised Claude Opus. This practice exposes users to serious privacy and security risks, including theft of proprietary code and business secrets, and undermines the legitimate AI service market by facilitating model fraud and illicit data harvesting for model distillation. The proxy operators obtain access via stolen credit cards, mass‑registered accounts, or by recruiting low‑wage workers in other countries to complete identity verification; they then either return results from cheaper models or harvest user prompts and outputs to resell as training data for model distillation.

**Background**: Anthropic’s Claude API provides programmatic access to its large language models, including the high‑end Claude Opus variant, which is normally sold at a premium price. In China, a grey‑market ecosystem of proxy services has emerged that resells this access through unofficial channels, often using fraudulent means such as stolen credentials or recruited verifiers to keep costs low. These proxies frequently substitute the requested model with cheaper or locally developed models and harvest users’ prompts and outputs, which can be used for model distillation—a technique that trains a smaller model on the outputs of a larger one to replicate its capabilities.
